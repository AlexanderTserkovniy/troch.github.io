First step and transition direction

Depending on the animation you want to perform, you might want to have access to more information about a step transition. For example, if you have a sliding animation like this current example, you want to know if you are transitioning to the next step or to the previous step. That way you can slide left the leaving step and slide from the right the entering step if it is a forward transition, and the opposite if it is a backward transition.

Also you might want to treat the first loaded step as a special case, either by not performing a transition or performing a different effect.

    The following classes are added to .multi-step-body element:
  • .step-initial for the first loaded step by the directive
  • .step-forward for a transition going forwards. This class is not added for the initial step
  • .step-backward for a transition going backwards.
